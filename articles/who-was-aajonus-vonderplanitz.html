Who Was Aajonus Vonderplanitz?

PrimalMail · Issue 01

Aajonus Vonderplanitz, 1970 — public domain (Wikimedia Commons)

Okay, first issue, so let's get the obvious question out of the way: who is this guy whose name is basically the whole premise of this newsletter?

Short version — Aajonus Vonderplanitz wrote a book called We Want to Live, built something called the Primal Diet around raw meat, raw dairy, and raw eggs, and died in 2013. Longer version is a lot weirder than that, so here's the longer version.

Not even his real name

He was born John Richard Swigart, Denver, 1947. "Aajonus" got picked up later — he took it from a toddler he met in his twenties, which is a strange enough detail that I had to double check it was real. Then he added "Vonderplanitz," a European surname from further back in his family, mostly because he just didn't like the name he was born with. I don't have a clean explanation for why a toddler's name specifically, and neither does anything I've read.

The part before the diet

He wasn't a doctor. No medical or scientific credentials of any kind — by his own account, self-taught. What got him there was a genuinely bad run of health: juvenile diabetes at 15, a stomach ulcer surgery that left a scar, then radiation to treat that scar which he said triggered bone marrow cancer. Chemo made things worse — psoriasis, more complications — so he stopped. Said raw carrot juice and raw dairy were what actually turned it around. I can't verify that part obviously, nobody can, but it's the origin story he told for decades.

The jackrabbit thing

Here's the detail that made me actually take this seriously instead of scrolling past it. He didn't start with raw meat — he was raw vegan first. The switch, by his account, happened while fasting alone in the wilderness in his late twenties: he watched wild dogs kill a jackrabbit, and they left him a piece of it. Raw. That's the moment, as he tells it, everything changed. Make of that what you will. It's either the most on-the-nose origin story in diet history or it actually happened exactly like that.

What was actually in it

The full thing is laid out in We Want to Live (1997, expanded 2005), but stripped down, it's this:

  • Raw meat and organ meats, never cooked
  • Raw, unpasteurized milk, cream, cheese
  • Raw eggs, usually blended into something
  • Raw animal fat as the main fuel source
  • Basically nothing cooked or processed
  • Sun exposure treated as part of the diet, not a risk to manage

And he didn't just publish and move on — he started a nonprofit (Right to Choose Healthy Foods) and set up private food-buying clubs so people could actually get raw dairy and meat. Reportedly dozens of these ran nationwide, some with over a thousand members. For a long stretch he was one of the loudest people pushing back on "everything must be cooked" as a food-safety default.

Why any of this matters to a newsletter in 2026

He died in 2013. The ideas didn't. Every raw-meat, anti-vegan creator getting traction right now — Goatis included — is standing on ground he cleared first, whether or not they'd ever say his name. That's reason enough to start here.
