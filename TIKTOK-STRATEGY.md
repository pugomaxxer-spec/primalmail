# PrimalMail — TikTok Promotion Plan

Goal: use TikTok as the discovery engine that funnels viewers into free
PrimalMail email subscribers, who then see ads/sponsors in the newsletter.

## Positioning
Account is a **commentary/curation account**, not a re-upload of Goatis's
content. Reacting to and discussing raw-meat/primal content is fine;
re-posting his videos without permission is not — don't do that.

## Content pillars (rotate across these)
1. **Reaction/commentary** — react to raw-meat or anti-vegan clips already
   circulating (including ones about Goatis), add your own take.
2. **Explainer** — 30–45s breakdowns of one Primal Diet rule at a time
   (raw dairy, raw eggs, raw fat) pulled from the newsletter articles.
3. **"Read the full breakdown"** — teaser hook that ends on a cliffhanger
   ("...and that's the part almost nobody talks about — full breakdown is
   in this week's PrimalMail, link in bio").
4. **Myth vs. claim** — "Here's what mainstream nutrition says vs. what the
   Primal Diet says" side-by-side format.

## Hook formulas that work for this niche
- "Nobody tells you this about raw dairy..."
- "This is why [X] is going raw in 2026"
- "I read Aajonus Vonderplanitz's book so you don't have to"
- "Goatis gets clowned for this but here's what he's actually saying"

## Funnel mechanics
- Bio link → PrimalMail signup page (`index.html#subscribe`)
- Every video CTA: "full breakdown + sources in the newsletter, link in bio"
- Pin a comment with the newsletter link on every video
- Post a "new issue is up" video each week the newsletter goes out

## Cadence
- 4–6 short videos/week to start; consistency matters more than volume
- Repurpose each newsletter issue into 2–3 short videos

## Before you monetize
Google AdSense (and most ad networks) restrict health content that makes
unproven medical claims or promotes practices they classify as dangerous
(raw dairy/meat, anti-vaccine framing both fall in gray/restricted zones).
Before relying on AdSense specifically, check their current health-content
and "dangerous or derogatory content" policies — you may want a backup plan
(direct sponsorships, affiliate links to raw-dairy/meat suppliers) in case
AdSense limits or rejects the site.
